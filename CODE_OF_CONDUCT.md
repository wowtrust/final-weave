# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

FinalWeave welcomes constructive technical participation. Contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and community members must help create a professional environment where people can challenge designs rigorously without attacking one another.

Expected behavior includes:

- discussing evidence, assumptions, threat models, and trade-offs clearly;
- giving actionable review feedback and receiving it in good faith;
- acknowledging uncertainty, prior work, sources, and other contributors;
- respecting different experience levels, identities, backgrounds, and communication styles;
- keeping security-sensitive or personal information in the appropriate private channel;
- correcting mistakes transparently and avoiding misleading implementation or performance claims.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, personal insults, sustained disruption, doxxing, publishing private correspondence without permission, deliberate security harm, or retaliation against good-faith reporters and reviewers.

## Scope

This standard applies in the repository, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, project spaces, release channels, community events, and public interactions where someone represents FinalWeave.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock conversations, reject contributions, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants when behavior threatens the community or project.

For ordinary moderation, contact the maintainers through the repository. For a confidential safety, harassment, privacy, or conduct report, email the [project maintainer](mailto:wangsiyuan19990317@gmail.com). Suspected software or protocol vulnerabilities must instead use the private security process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Reports will be evaluated in context. Maintainers should protect reporter privacy where possible, avoid conflicts of interest, document enforcement decisions, and apply consequences consistently and proportionally.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by established open-source community standards and is maintained as part of FinalWeave's repository governance.
